Trump Fires Attorney General Bondi Over Epstein Files: What's His Real Agenda?

Anka Research — April 08, 2026 — Daily Intelligence

In a stunning move that has sent shockwaves through Washington, President Trump fired Attorney General Pam Bondi on April 2, 2026, with sources indicating her handling of the Epstein files played a central role in her dismissal (CNN, Fox, WashPost, Al Jazeera). Todd Blanche now serves as Acting Attorney General, raising critical questions about what comes next (CBS News).

According to NBC News, Trump was frustrated that Bondi "wasn't aggressive enough" on the Epstein files, while The Washington Post reports he also wanted more aggressive prosecution of his political enemies. But this raises a disturbing question: if Trump thought Bondi was too soft on Epstein-related matters, what exactly is his agenda with these files?

The Department of Justice has already published a staggering 3.5 million responsive pages in what they're calling the "Epstein Library" and released the Epstein Files Transparency Act Section 3 Report (DOJ.gov). PBS has published the full list of powerful people named in these files, including several Indian businessmen and political figures that have drawn international attention.

Yet despite this unprecedented document dump, no new criminal charges have been filed. Democratic lawmakers had written to Bondi before her firing, demanding stronger victim advocacy (House Oversight), while survivors continue asking the fundamental question: where is the accountability? (Iowa Public Radio).

Bondi's firing sends a chilling message. If she wasn't aggressive enough for Trump's liking, was she actually trying to pursue justice, or was Trump expecting her to weaponize the files for political purposes? The President's frustration with her approach to both the Epstein matter and prosecuting political enemies suggests a dangerous conflation of personal vendettas with justice.

Todd Blanche now holds the keys to one of the most sensitive investigations in modern history. Will he prove tougher on actual perpetrators, or will he simply be another cover for Trump's broader agenda? The international implications are massive, with Indian media outlets tracking connections to prominent figures including the Ambani family and other business leaders mentioned in the files.

The real test will be whether Blanche treats these files as evidence for justice or as ammunition for political warfare. Bondi's dismissal suggests Trump views the Epstein case through a lens of personal utility rather than criminal justice.

**What to Watch:** Blanche's first moves as Acting Attorney General, any new criminal referrals from the Epstein files, and whether Lee Zeldin (being considered as permanent replacement per CNBC) would take an even more politicized approach to this investigation that demands actual justice, not political theater.
