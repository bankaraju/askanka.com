Iran-US Week 6: Oil, Defence & the FII Exodus

Anka Research — April 4, 2026 — 5 min read

As we enter the sixth week of military escalation between Iran and the United States, the market impact is becoming increasingly pronounced across multiple sectors. The conflict's ripple effects are now clearly visible in Indian equity markets, creating both opportunities and risks for spread traders.

The Hormuz Bottleneck

The most immediate concern remains the Strait of Hormuz disruptions. Major shipping companies continue to reroute oil tankers via the Cape of Good Hope, adding approximately 15 days to delivery times. Source: Reuters

This bottleneck has pushed Brent crude consistently above $95 per barrel, with supply fears outweighing demand concerns. For Indian markets, this translates directly into pressure on oil marketing companies. IOC, BPCL, and HPCL have all seen margin compression as India's crude import bill rises sharply. Source: Economic Times

The OMCs are caught between rising input costs and regulated retail fuel prices — a classic squeeze that our spread recommendations have been positioned to capture since Week 2.

Defence Outperformance

On the defence side, we're seeing a clear outperformance pattern. HAL, BEL, and BDL have all posted significant gains as regional security premiums drive defence spending expectations higher. The Trump administration's escalating rhetoric, particularly from Pentagon briefings led by Defence Secretary Hegseth, suggests this conflict may extend longer than markets initially priced in. Source: AP News

This creates both momentum opportunities and valuation concerns that our spread strategies — specifically Defence vs IT and Defence vs Auto — are designed to capture.

FII Flight & Selective Opportunities

The foreign institutional investor response has been predictably negative for broader market sentiment. FII outflows from Indian equities accelerated this week as geopolitical risk premiums rise. However, this selling pressure is creating selective opportunities in fundamentally strong names caught in the broader risk-off sentiment. Source: Economic Times

The Gulf Neutrality Question

What's particularly interesting from a regional perspective is the stance of Gulf states. Both UAE and Saudi Arabia continue to maintain official neutrality despite pressure from Washington and Tehran. This positioning is crucial for India given our substantial diaspora in these regions and our energy relationships. Source: Al Jazeera

Any shift in Gulf state positions could significantly impact both India's energy security and remittance flows. Our ARCBE engine is tracking Gulf-exposed Indian equities (Sobha, Aster DM, L&T, InterGlobe) against domestic-pure names for exactly this scenario.

Sanctions & Supply Chain

The sanctions landscape is also evolving rapidly. The US Treasury is preparing additional financial restrictions targeting Iranian oil exports, which could further tighten global supply. For Indian refiners who have historically relied on Iranian crude, this creates both supply chain challenges and opportunities to source from alternative suppliers — potentially at premium pricing. Source: Reuters

Rupee Resilience — For Now

The rupee has shown remarkable resilience despite broader emerging market weakness, largely due to RBI intervention and strong services exports. However, the rising oil import bill poses medium-term current account pressures that markets are beginning to price in. Source: Economic Times

Week 7 Outlook

Diplomatic channels remain largely stalled. Al Jazeera's coverage suggests that both sides maintain hardline positions, reinforcing our view that elevated volatility and risk premiums are likely to persist through the coming weeks.

Key variables to monitor: escalation in shipping disruptions, Trump administration policy announcements, and most critically — any signs of Gulf state position changes that could affect India's energy security calculus.

The geopolitical landscape remains fluid, but the market impacts are increasingly clear and tradeable through disciplined spread strategies. Our current recommendations focus on Upstream vs Downstream (long ONGC/OIL, short IOC/BPCL) and Defence vs IT (long HAL/BEL, short TCS/INFY).
