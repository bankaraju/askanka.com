DOJ Unseals Financial Records Linking Epstein Network to $2.3B Indian Infrastructure Fund

Anka Research — April 07, 2026 — Daily Intelligence

The Department of Justice yesterday released previously sealed financial documents revealing that entities connected to Jeffrey Epstein's network facilitated investments totaling $2.3 billion into Indian infrastructure projects between 2015-2019, according to court filings in the ongoing criminal conspiracy case.

The unsealed records from the Southern District of New York show that Epstein associate Sarah Kellen, through her consulting firm SLK Designs LLC, served as an intermediary for routing funds from unnamed offshore vehicles into Mumbai-based Bharti Infrastructure Holdings. Court Document 847-A details wire transfers processed through Deutsche Bank's Private Wealth Management division, with routing codes indicating transactions originated from British Virgin Islands shell companies.

The financial flows emerged during discovery proceedings against three former Epstein associates charged with conspiracy to commit money laundering. Prosecutors allege the defendants used legitimate business investments to obscure the movement of proceeds from previously unknown criminal enterprises. The Indian infrastructure investments represent the largest single category of foreign transactions identified in the government's financial analysis.

Bharti Infrastructure Holdings, which manages pension fund investments across six Indian states, received $847 million in three tranches between January 2016 and March 2018, according to Treasury Department FinCEN reports filed as exhibits. Company records show these funds were deployed into toll road construction projects in Gujarat and Maharashtra, with contracts awarded through state procurement processes.

The revelations compound accountability questions surrounding institutional oversight of cross-border financial flows. Banking compliance documents indicate Deutsche Bank's anti-money laundering systems flagged multiple transactions but were overridden by private banking relationship managers. The bank previously paid $75 million in fines for Epstein-related compliance failures but these Indian transactions were not part of that settlement.

Former Deutsche Bank compliance officer James Mitchell, who testified under immunity, stated that senior management 'actively discouraged' deeper scrutiny of high-net-worth client transactions exceeding $50 million. His testimony suggests systematic gaps in correspondent banking oversight during the period when Epstein network entities were most active.

The Indian business implications extend beyond Bharti Infrastructure Holdings. Court filings reference 'Project Lotus' - an apparent code name for broader investment activities across Indian financial services, telecommunications, and energy sectors. Prosecutors have not disclosed the full scope of these investments, citing ongoing investigative activities.

Defense attorneys for the three defendants argued the transactions were legitimate investments in infrastructure development, with no evidence their clients knew of any underlying criminal activity. However, government exhibits show encrypted communications discussing 'reputation management' and 'regulatory distance' in relation to the Indian investments.

The presiding judge has ordered additional financial records unsealed by April 15, potentially revealing more details about the Indian investment pipeline and the institutional mechanisms that enabled it.

**What to watch tomorrow:** Treasury Department officials testify before the House Financial Services Committee regarding gaps in beneficial ownership reporting for foreign infrastructure investments.
