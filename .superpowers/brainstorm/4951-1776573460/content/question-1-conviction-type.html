What kind of "conviction" are we filtering?

Your system has two very different conviction metrics. They answer different questions.

A

TA Pattern Win Rates

"Show me stocks where a pattern fires with >70% historical win rate"

Example: RELIANCE — ATR_COMPRESSION has 72% win rate over 5 days, occurred 45 times in 5 years, avg return +2.1%.

This is what you described: click a stock → see the chart with pattern markers → narration says "this happened 5 times in 4 years and usually made X% returns."

Data available: 213 stocks × 15 patterns each, with win_rate_5d, avg_return_5d, occurrences, significance, direction.

B

Spread / Regime Conviction

"Show me spreads where regime win rate is >70% right now"

Example: Defence vs IT — 73% win rate in EUPHORIA over 5 days. This is the regime engine output.

Fewer items (~10-15 eligible spreads), changes daily with regime shifts. More about "what to trade today" than stock exploration.

Data available: eligible_spreads from today_regime.json with 1d/3d/5d win rates.

C

Both — Unified Scanner

"Show me everything above a threshold, any source"

Combines TA patterns + spread conviction into one filterable list. Ambitious but potentially confusing — apples and oranges in one basket.

Risk: a 70% TA win rate on a single pattern (backward-looking) is NOT the same as a 70% spread conviction (regime-conditional, forward-looking). Mixing them could mislead.

MY PUSH-BACK

Option C sounds appealing but mixes two fundamentally different signals. A stock with a 75% TA win rate hammer pattern doesn't mean "buy it" — it means that specific pattern has historically worked. The spread conviction is regime-dependent and changes daily. Putting them in one list with a single threshold slider creates false equivalence.

My recommendation: Option A — it matches what you described (click stock → chart → narration) and we have rich data for all 213 stocks. Spread conviction already lives in the Trading → Spreads tab where it belongs.
