1. Cadence: Every Intraday Cycle (15-min, 26x/day)
Decision: Approved.
Rationale: The system already operates on a high-fidelity 15-minute IST clockwork schedule for Stations 7 and 8
. Standardising the MSI refresh to match the intraday cycle (26 times per day) ensures the "Brain" remains synchronized without introducing new, asynchronous moving parts
.
2. Partial-Failure Policy: Hold Morning MSI and Show Stale Dot
Decision: Approved (Option b).
Rationale: Displaying a partial or potentially skewed MSI number violates the core principle of Truth-Anchored Integrity
. In line with the Staleness Awareness UX pattern, if any of the 4 intraday APIs fail, the system must hold the validated morning MSI and trigger the Watchdog amber alert to signal that the intraday recompute is not fresh
.
3. Hysteresis: No Intraday Re-evaluation
Decision: Approved.
Rationale: The 2-day Hysteresis Rule is structurally designed as a 2-session (day-over-day) cycle to prevent "whipsawing" caused by temporary noise
. Since MSI is currently treated as secondary context to the primary ETF-derived regime, allowing it to trigger intraday resets of the "Stable" count would undermine the system's operational discipline
.
4. Scope Boundary: Strict MSI Refresh + Banner Honesty
Decision: Approved.
Rationale: The primary goal of this task is to ensure that the numbers shown to the practitioner are "honest" and live
. Expanding the scope to include intraday updates for eligible_spreads or Phase B picks represents a major shift in the "Regime-to-Trades Mapping" logic, which currently relies on a frozen trade map
. That change deserves its own specialized research and validation spec
.
