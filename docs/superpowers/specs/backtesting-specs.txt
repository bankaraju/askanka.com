BACKTESTING STANDARDS — ANKA RESEARCH
Revision 1.1 (2026-05-03). Governing document for every backtest, paper trade and live strategy launch on the Anka platform. Every strategy must pass every applicable gate below before it touches real capital. Any deviation requires a written waiver from the principal (Bharat) logged in docs/superpowers/waivers/ before the deviation takes effect. Section 0 principles cannot be waived.

Next mandatory review: 2027-04-23 (annual cycle).


0. RESEARCH INTEGRITY PRINCIPLES
These are the rules we will not break even when they are inconvenient.

0.1 We do not publish a strategy as "deployable" until it has passed every applicable gate in Sections 1-15. Partial passes are labelled "Research" and are forbidden from the live signal path. Section 15 governs the promotion mechanism; Sections 1-14 define the gate criteria.

0.2 We do not retroactively redefine success criteria to make a losing strategy pass. If a strategy fails its pre-registered hypothesis (Section 14), it is retired — we may rebuild under a new hypothesis but not rename the failure.

0.3 We do not adjust p-value thresholds to fit discoveries. Bonferroni and FDR corrections are applied to the full hypothesis family as scoped at pre-registration (see Section 14.5), not to the surviving subset. A strategy that stops being significant after multiplicity correction is not significant.

0.4 We do not trust a single-point backtest. Every deployable claim is tested at every slippage level (S0-S2 minimum), across every regime (3 of 4 minimum), on a held-out out-of-sample segment, and validated forward for a specified minimum in shadow before live.

0.5 We do not hide rejected hypotheses. Section 14's registry tracks every strategy ever tested, deployed or killed, with reason. This is the only defence against the garden of forking paths.

0.6 We do not deploy faster than the data can justify. A strategy that cleanly passes Sections 1-14 but fails Section 15's shadow-period requirement waits. "Market-open urgency" is not a reason to skip forward validation.

0.7 We document every decision. Every deployment, waiver, parameter change and post-mortem lands in git with a dated commit message. The principal's ego and the researcher's enthusiasm are not valid auditors.

0.8 We apply these principles recursively. A tool that evaluates strategies (e.g., the unified backtest itself) must also pass them — we backtest the backtester.


1. SLIPPAGE STRESS GRID
Objective: Measure how robust each strategy and each Phase C class (especially OPPORTUNITY) is under increasingly pessimistic execution costs.

1.1 Slippage levels (bps, one-way)
- S0 (Base): exchange + broker + taxes as currently modeled, plus 5 bps slippage per side (10 bps round-trip).
- S1 (Moderate stress): base costs plus 10 bps extra slippage per side (~30 bps round-trip).
- S2 (High stress): base costs plus 20 bps extra slippage per side (~50 bps round-trip).
- S3 (Extreme, optional): base costs plus 30 bps extra slippage per side (~70 bps round-trip).

1.2 Fill simulator requirement
For buys: effective price = mid/last + slippage_bps × price. For sells: effective price = mid/last − slippage_bps × price. slippage_bps is a parameter; every evaluation runs across [0.0005, 0.0010, 0.0020, 0.0030].


2. METRICS PER SLIPPAGE LEVEL
For each slippage level, compute the following at portfolio and per-strategy scope.

2.1 Per-strategy and portfolio
- Annualized Sharpe (net of all costs)
- Annualized volatility
- Max drawdown (%)
- Calmar (CAGR / Max DD)
- Hit rate (winning trades / total trades)
- Average R multiple (avg win, avg loss, win/loss ratio)
- Trade count and turnover (notional per day)

2.2 Per Phase C class
For each Phase C label (OPPORTUNITY, POSSIBLE_OPPORTUNITY, WARNING, CONFIRMED_WARNING, UNCERTAIN):
- Mean next-bar and next-N-bars return
- Hit rate for direction (where applicable)
- Annualized Sharpe for trades originating from that label
- Contribution to total P&L and drawdown


3. PASS / FAIL CRITERIA UNDER STRESS
Moderate baseline: Sharpe ≥ 1.0, DD ≤ 20%, hit ≥ 55%.

3.1 At S0 (Base) — OPPORTUNITY trades must satisfy
- Sharpe (net) ≥ 1.0
- Hit rate ≥ 55%
- Max DD ≤ 20%
- Positive P&L and Sharpe ≥ 0.5 in at least 3 of 4 regimes
- p-value ≤ 0.05, Bonferroni-adjusted against the Section 14.5 primary hypothesis family

If it fails S0 the strategy is Not Deployable.

3.2 At S1 (Moderate, +10 bps per side)
- Sharpe (net) ≥ 0.8
- Max DD ≤ 25%
- Edge present (Sharpe ≥ 0.5) in ≥ 2 of 4 regimes
- Positive cumulative P&L over full backtest horizon

Fail S1 → flagged "Fragile to realistic slippage"; research only, cannot size aggressively.

3.3 At S2 (High, +20 bps per side)
- Sharpe (net) ≥ 0.5
- Max DD ≤ 30%
- Positive cumulative P&L overall

Fail S2 → edge is execution-dependent; run only in most liquid contexts with conservative sizing.

3.4 At S3 (Extreme) — information only; no strict bar.


4. REPORTING / DASHBOARD
Track Record / Proof tab must include a Slippage Resilience section.

4.1 Per-strategy resilience table
Columns S0, S1, S2, (S3); rows Sharpe, Max DD, CAGR, Hit Rate, Net P&L.

4.2 Per-label informational value
Rows: Phase C labels. Columns: mean next-bar return, Sharpe, hit rate at S0 and S2.


5. INTEGRATION INTO SUNDAY UNIFIED BACKTEST
5.1 Parameterize slippage in unified_backtest.py.
5.2 For each weekly Sunday run: run the full walk-forward once per slippage level (S0-S2, S3 optional); persist metrics per strategy and per Phase C label; apply pass/fail logic.
5.3 Classification outcome per the ladder in Section 15.1.
5.4 Display current classification and last-run metrics on the Track Record / Proof tab.


5A. DATA QUALITY AND CORPORATE-ACTION INTEGRITY
Objective: Ensure no strategy passes because the underlying data is stale, malformed, inconsistently adjusted, or silently repaired.

5A.1 Mandatory data audit per run
Every backtest emits a data-quality report containing, at minimum, per symbol and in aggregate:
- missing_bar_count
- duplicate_timestamp_count
- stale_quote_count (unchanged OHLC for > N expected bars)
- zero_or_negative_price_count
- zero_volume_bar_count where trading should have occurred
- corporate_action_events (split, bonus, dividend, symbol change, merger, demerger)
- adjusted_series_mode used at entry and exit

5A.2 Adjustment consistency
A strategy must use one consistent price convention across entry, exit, stops, labels and reporting:
- adjusted everywhere, or
- unadjusted everywhere with explicit corporate-action handling.
Mixed conventions are forbidden. Specifically: z-score tests on raw price levels must use unadjusted series; z-score tests on returns must use split-adjusted series; never mix conventions inside one strategy.

5A.3 Bad-data budget
Thresholds calibrated to Indian equity baseline (NSE holidays, circuit filters and technical halts generate 1-2% stale/missing bars in a typical 5-year window):
- > 1.0% impaired: tag DATA-IMPAIRED (strategy may proceed to research but not to deployment without a waiver)
- > 3.0% impaired: auto-fail; cannot be used for deployment review under any waiver

5A.4 Repair log
Any repair, forward-fill, symbol map, corporate-action splice or exclusion must be logged to docs/superpowers/data-audits/YYYY-MM-DD-<run>.md with reason and code path. Silent repair is forbidden.

5A.5 Raw-bar canonicity (policy reference)
Observed OHLC is canonical on the authoritative compliance path. Any bar flagged by §5A.1 inside a trade's execution window (per §7.1 mode binding) invalidates that trade — no substitution, no imputation, no silent pass-through. Imputation, if used, runs only on a parallel sensitivity track emitting a distinct artifact (`metrics_grid_sensitivity.json`); the §15.1 gate never reads the sensitivity track. Full definition, sector-beta imputer formula, and logging obligations: docs/superpowers/policies/2026-04-23-raw-bar-canonicity.md. This policy implements §5A.4 as an execution gate.


6. SURVIVORSHIP AND UNIVERSE INTEGRITY
Objective: Ensure the stock universe available to the backtest on any historical date matches the universe that was actually tradeable on that date.

6.1 Point-in-time universe construction
Maintain pipeline/data/fno_universe_history.json with monthly F&O-member snapshots going back at least 5 years. When the backtest evaluates a signal on date T, only tickers in the F&O list as of T are eligible. Tickers delisted or kicked out between T and now must still appear in historical results up to their exit date.

6.2 Mandatory disclosures per run
Every backtest report must state:
- n_tickers_current (today's universe)
- n_tickers_ever (total unique tickers appearing 2021-now)
- n_tickers_delisted
- coverage_ratio = n_delisted / n_tickers_ever
If coverage_ratio < 10% of the ever-universe OR the backtest silently uses today's universe only, the report is tagged SURVIVORSHIP-UNCORRECTED and strategies built on it cannot cross Section 15 without a written waiver.

6.3 Data provider
Maintain a fetcher that pulls monthly NSE F&O membership changes and diff-appends to fno_universe_history.json. Watchdog alerts if the file is >35 days stale.


7. ENTRY / EXIT TIMING DISCIPLINE
Objective: Eliminate every form of look-ahead at the trade boundary. If the backtest models a fill at price P at time t, the strategy must have been able to know-and-act on that fill at time t in real life.

7.1 Execution modes
Every strategy declares one mode; the fill simulator mirrors it.

MODE A — EOD-close entry, close-to-close hold
- Signal finalised by 15:15 IST using only data available at or before 15:15
- Fill price = 15:20-15:29 VWAP (±5 bps variance tolerance) in SSF
- Exit at subsequent close (15:20-15:29 VWAP of exit day) or on intraday stop
- No open-snap. No next-morning-rewrite. The ledger price equals what the exchange printed in the 15:20-29 window.

MODE B — Morning-settled entry, intraday hold
- Signal generated overnight or pre-market
- Fill NOT at 09:15 open. Fill price = 09:45 LTP (30-minute post-open settling rule) or later
- Rationale: pre-open auction imbalance can move prices 0.5-2% on noise

MODE C — Intraday adaptive
- Signal generated intraday at time t; fill price = t+5 minute VWAP
- Minimum 30 minutes after open; no fills after 15:20

7.2 Forbidden practices
- snap_entry_to_market_open-style functions that rewrite the ledger after price moves are banned in the live path. Only allowed in a labelled "theoretical-alpha" research report, never in a deployed strategy's P&L.
- Using today's close as the entry price for a signal that fired intraday today.
- Mixed adjustment conventions across entry and exit (see 5A.2).

7.3 Audit hook
Every backtest emits an entry-timing audit log: per-trade signal-decidable timestamp, filled-price timestamp, and lag. If lag < 0 (fill before signal is decidable) OR lag < 30 min in MODE B/C, the backtest fails this section.


8. STRATEGY-DIRECTION AUDIT
Objective: Detect and flag cases where the live engine fires a trade in one direction but the backtest validates the opposite direction as the edge.

8.1 Direction declaration
Every strategy emits, per signal, { symbol, timestamp, direction: LONG|SHORT, thesis: peer-follow | mean-reversion | trend-continuation | news-catalyst | … }.

8.2 Backtest-vs-engine cross-check
The unified backtest re-runs every live-shipping strategy on historical data AND separately runs the opposite-direction strategy for comparison. Report: for the same event set, Sharpe / PnL of direction=LONG vs direction=SHORT. If opposite-direction Sharpe exceeds the strategy's own Sharpe at S0, the strategy is stamped DIRECTION-SUSPECT and must resolve before next release.

8.3 Naming discipline
A strategy named "fade-the-overshoot" that internally follows peer direction is a lie. Name mismatches are a P0 bug.


9. MINIMUM SAMPLE SIZE AND STATISTICAL POWER
Objective: Prevent tiny-n strategies from being stamped "significant" by chance.

9.1 Minimum trade count
- n ≥ 30 trades per regime for single-ticker strategies
- n ≥ 50 trades total for baskets or rule families
- n ≥ 100 events if the strategy is being backtested alone (no multiplicity correction planned)

9.2 Power analysis
Before pre-registering a hypothesis (Section 14), compute the minimum detectable effect size given available n at alpha=0.05 and power=0.80. Under-powered hypotheses are labelled exploratory only.

9.3 Confidence intervals
Every reported edge must include a 95% bootstrap CI (10,000 resamples from event-level returns). A 1.5% edge with a [-0.5%, 3.5%] CI is not a usable signal regardless of the mean.


9A. PARAMETER FRAGILITY AND LOCAL STABILITY
Objective: Detect strategies that pass only because one exact parameter combination happened to fit historical noise.

9A.1 Local neighborhood test
For every deployable parameterized strategy, the backtest evaluates a local grid around the chosen parameter set. Required resolution: ≥ 9 evaluated points per parameter axis, ≥ 25 total neighborhood samples. Default perturbation ranges:
- lookbacks: ±20%
- stops / targets: ±20%
- hold horizons: ±1 bar if short horizon, ±20% otherwise
- thresholds / z-scores: ±10%

9A.2 Stability requirements — all three must hold
- At least 60% of neighboring parameter points preserve positive net P&L
- Median neighboring Sharpe ≥ 70% of the chosen-point Sharpe
- No majority of neighboring points exhibit opposite-direction inversion

9A.3 Fragility flag
If the chosen point materially outperforms the neighborhood but the neighborhood is weak or sign-flipping, the strategy is stamped PARAMETER-FRAGILE and cannot progress past RESEARCH without a written waiver.


9B. NULL MODEL, PERMUTATION AND REALITY-CHECK TESTS
Objective: Ensure the claimed edge exceeds what could be obtained from random timing, label luck, or selection among many candidate rules.

9B.1 Naive benchmark requirement
Every strategy is compared against all applicable comparators chosen pre-registration from:
- Random direction on the same event set
- Same-direction sector or index baseline
- Simple momentum / reversal rule using one headline variable only
- Equal-weight event basket with no ranking

Pass condition: strategy primary metric must exceed the STRONGEST of its naive comparators at S0 on the same event set. Beating only the weakest is not a pass.

9B.2 Permutation test
For each strategy-version, run permutations preserving event count and, where relevant, temporal dependence structure. Acceptable methods: block bootstrap, label permutation, event-time shuffle, White's Reality Check, Hansen SPA.

Minimum permutation counts — required resolution depends on the significance threshold needed:
- No multiplicity correction applied (alpha=0.05): ≥ 1,000 permutations
- Bonferroni or FDR in effect: ≥ 100,000 permutations, OR use an analytical / asymptotic p-value with stated assumptions
Rationale: at 1,000 permutations the finest resolvable p-value is 0.001. Bonferroni-adjusted alpha at realistic family sizes (e.g., 422 tests → 1.2e-4) is unreachable at that resolution.

9B.3 Pass condition
Observed strategy performance must exceed the 95th percentile of the null distribution on the pre-registered primary metric. If not, the strategy is labelled NULL-CONSISTENT and is not deployable regardless of raw backtest Sharpe.

9B.4 Multiplicity interaction
Permutation / reality-check significance does not replace Bonferroni or FDR. Both must be passed where applicable, at the permutation counts required by 9B.2.


9C. CELL-LEVEL QUALIFIER GATE STANDARDS
Added 2026-05-03 (Revision 1.1) after H-2026-05-04-cross-asset-perstock-lasso-v1 surfaced a 6-gate qualifier in which 4 gates were non-standard or in violation of §9B.2. This section codifies what counts as a valid cell-level qualifier gate for any classifier-based hypothesis (per-stock, per-direction, per-rule). It binds future hypotheses; in-flight holdouts ride out their original gates per §10.4 and are evaluated retrospectively under this standard at verdict time.

9C.1 Two-gate canonical qualifier
Every classifier-based hypothesis that promotes per-cell models to a tradeable basket must clear, at minimum:
- GATE A — mean walk-forward fold-AUC ≥ 0.53 (intraday equity classification with low transaction costs; threshold aligns with López de Prado AFML 2018 §7.4 academic median 0.52-0.54)
- GATE B — multiple-comparison-corrected p-value below pre-registered alpha (Bonferroni, BH-FDR, or White's Reality Check) where the family scope is declared at pre-registration per §14.5 and the permutation count satisfies §9B.2 (≥100,000 when FDR/Bonferroni is in effect)

A cell that clears Gate A and Gate B is a tradeable cell. The basket assembled from tradeable cells is then evaluated against §3.1 (S0/S1 deployment criteria) and §12 (edge stability).

9C.2 Forbidden cell-level gates
The following are explicitly forbidden as cell-level qualifier gates because they are non-standard (no published reference uses them as binding gates), redundant with Gates A or B, or vulnerable to look-ahead leakage:
- fold-AUC standard deviation as a hard gate (informational only — convention: report std, do not gate)
- in-sample-holdout AUC as a gate when hyperparameters are cross-validated on the full panel (creates double-jeopardy with the walk-forward folds AND leaks the holdout into hyperparameter selection; use a true expanding-rolling holdout or drop the gate)
- absolute predicted-positive count thresholds (n_pred_pos at p≥k for arbitrary k); use percentile-based ranking instead, or rely on Gate B alone for false-discovery control
- "permutation-beat-percentile ≥ 95%" as a separate gate when BH-FDR (Gate B) is already in place — these measure the same null and are redundant

9C.3 Family scope
Per §14.5 the multiple-comparison family must be declared at pre-registration. For per-stock, per-direction classifier hypotheses the default scope is per-direction (LONG and SHORT each form their own family) — they target different market mechanisms (uptrend vs downtrend dynamics) and pooling them inflates the BH-FDR denominator without justification. Pooling LONG + SHORT into one family requires a written justification in the pre-registration entry, not a default.

9C.4 In-flight holdout discipline
This standard does not apply mechanically to hypotheses already in their holdout window per §10.4 strict — those ride out their pre-registered gates. At verdict time, the unified backtest emits a "retrospective gate sensitivity" measurement (cells that would have qualified under §9C) alongside the actual verdict. The retrospective measurement is informational only and does not change the verdict.

9C.5 Reporting
Every classifier-based hypothesis report must include:
- per-cell mean fold-AUC, fold std, and BH-FDR p-value
- count of cells passing Gate A only, Gate B only, and joint
- per-direction BH-FDR family size and rank-1 threshold
- N_PERMUTATIONS used and whether it satisfies §9B.2 for the family size

9C.6 Waiver path
Any pre-registration that proposes additional cell-level gates beyond §9C.1 (e.g., for novel signal types where Gate A's intraday-AUC threshold is inappropriate) requires a written waiver per §15.4 specifying (a) what the additional gate measures, (b) the published or empirical evidence supporting the threshold, and (c) why Gates A and B are insufficient on their own.


10. OUT-OF-SAMPLE DISCIPLINE AND PURGED WALK-FORWARD
Objective: Ensure no researcher sees the evaluation segment during strategy development.

10.1 True holdout
A segment of at least 20% of available history, ending with the most recent 3 months, is the pure holdout. It is not loaded into any notebook, script or report during development. The strategy is scored on the holdout exactly once after all tuning is complete. On failure, the old holdout is burned; a new one must be carved from even-more-recent data (typically requires waiting).

10.2 Walk-forward parameters
- Train window: 3 years (756 trading days) rolling
- Test window: 3 months (63 trading days) rolling
- Step: 1 month (21 trading days)
- Embargo: 5 days on either side of every train/test boundary

10.3 Purging for overlapping trades
Strategies that hold positions for > 1 day must purge training data overlapping with test-period events: if a test trade opens on T_test and closes on T_test+5, all training trades whose close is within [T_test − 5, T_test + 5] are dropped from training. López de Prado combinatorial purged cross-validation is the canonical reference.

10.4 Single-use rule for OOS
The holdout is scored exactly once per strategy-version. Re-runs require a version increment (v2, v3) with that version's OOS touch recorded. After 3 re-touches the holdout is burned; a new one must be established before further deployment.


11. LIQUIDITY AND CAPACITY
Objective: Ensure edges modelled on paper can be captured at the capital we intend to deploy, without the act of trading eroding the signal.

11.1 Average Daily Value threshold
Each ticker's 60-day ADV in ₹ notional must exceed 10× the intended position size. Positions beyond that are backtested with a linear impact penalty: slippage_bps += 5 × (position_size / ADV).

11.2 Capacity curve
Every deployable strategy reports the capacity curve: Sharpe, DD and net CAGR at position sizes {₹5 lakh, ₹25 lakh, ₹1 Cr, ₹5 Cr} per trade. The curve is disclosed on the Track Record / Proof tab.

11.3 Hard notional caps
At deployment, by classification:
- Not Deployable — max ₹0 (paper only)
- Fragile — max ₹5 lakh per trade, ₹25 lakh aggregate
- Execution-Sensitive — max ₹25 lakh per trade, ₹1 Cr aggregate
- Robust — max ₹1 Cr per trade, ₹5 Cr aggregate (re-review at this level)


11A. IMPLEMENTATION-RISK SIMULATION
Objective: Model the ugly parts of production that ordinary slippage stress does not capture.

11A.1 Failure scenarios
Every deployable strategy is stress-tested under, at minimum, all ten of the following:
1. 5% missed entries (randomly distributed)
2. 5% missed exits (randomly distributed) — held past stop until next bar
3. 5-minute delayed fills at entry
4. Stale signal snapshot at entry (one bar old)
5. Rejected exit order retried one bar later
6. Partial fill at 50% intended size
7. One upstream data / API outage per month, randomly timed
8. Exchange halt or circuit freeze at T+1 open — exit delayed to re-open or mid-session
9. Margin-shortage rejects during drawdown — entry blocked when running DD exceeds 10%
10. Weekend / holiday gap scenario for overnight-hold strategies: overnight move > 3× intraday volatility applied at open of day after non-trading period

11A.2 Pass condition
Under the combined scenario set a deployable strategy must satisfy all three:
- Cumulative P&L remains positive
- Max DD ≤ 1.4 × backtest Max DD at S1 (absolute ceiling, not class-relative)
- Realised Sharpe ≥ 60% of the S1 value

11A.3 Classification interaction
A strategy that passes Sections 1-3 but fails 11A is labelled IMPLEMENTATION-SENSITIVE and is capped one class lower than its slippage classification would otherwise permit.


11B. MARKET-CORRELATION AND ALPHA-AFTER-BETA
Objective: Distinguish genuine alpha from disguised beta / leverage.

11B.1 Benchmark-regression requirement
Every deployable strategy regresses its daily / trade-level returns against NIFTY-50 total returns and reports:
- Beta to NIFTY (slope)
- Alpha annualized (intercept × 252)
- R² of regression
- Sharpe of residual returns (after subtracting β × NIFTY return)

11B.2 Pass condition
- Residual Sharpe ≥ 70% of gross Sharpe at S0 (i.e., strategy retains most of its edge after stripping out market beta)
- |Beta| ≤ 0.6 for a strategy marketed as "market neutral"; otherwise beta must be disclosed in the Track Record tab
- Alpha after beta adjustment must pass the same Bonferroni-adjusted significance test as raw returns (Section 3.1)

11B.3 Disclosure
The Track Record tab reports beta, alpha and residual Sharpe for every deployed strategy. Investors see the decomposition, not only the headline return.


11C. PORTFOLIO CORRELATION GATE
Objective: Prevent a portfolio of strategies that are secretly all the same trade.

11C.1 Correlation test on promotion
Before a strategy is promoted from PAPER-SHADOW to LIVE-FRAGILE, its rolling 60-day P&L correlation to every already-deployed strategy is computed. Fail condition: any pairwise correlation > 0.60 with an existing deployed strategy.

11C.2 Portfolio-level aggregate risk
The deployed-strategy portfolio maintains a published correlation matrix updated weekly. If any new pair crosses 0.70 for four consecutive weeks, the newer of the two is demoted one level pending diversification review.

11C.3 Concentration cap
No more than 40% of portfolio Sharpe-weighted exposure may come from strategies sharing a primary signal family (e.g., "all residual-reversion", "all momentum-breakout"). Researchers must document family membership in the hypothesis registry (Section 14).


12. EDGE DECAY AND STATIONARITY
Objective: Detect when an edge that once existed has died.

12.1 Rolling edge plot
Every deployable strategy publishes a rolling 12-month Sharpe / Hit-rate / Edge-% plot; unified backtest refreshes weekly.

12.2 Regime-change detection
CUSUM control chart on rolling monthly P&L with a threshold of 3σ from backtest-period volatility. A trigger puts the strategy on decay-watch for 30 days. Two triggers in 90 days auto-suspends pending manual review.

12.3 Recent-years requirement
Backtest edge on the most recent 24 months must be ≥ 50% of the edge on full history. Strategies below the threshold are labelled DECAYING and cannot be new-deployed without a specific thesis for the anomaly.

12.4 Annual re-certification
Any strategy in LIVE-ROBUST status for > 12 calendar months re-runs the full Sections 1-14 checklist annually. Failure at re-certification demotes to LIVE-FRAGILE pending fix, regardless of current P&L.


13. PAPER-VS-LIVE DRIFT AUDIT
Objective: Catch implementation bugs by comparing shadow (paper) P&L to what the backtest predicted.

13.1 Weekly drift report
Every Sunday, for each strategy running in shadow or live:
- backtest_expected_pnl_sharpe (as of the last weekly walk-forward)
- shadow_realised_pnl_sharpe (since go-live or strategy start)
- drift_ratio = shadow / backtest_expected
If drift_ratio < 0.5 or > 2.0 over a rolling 60-trade window, alert. Either the implementation is broken or the backtest is sham (both P0).

13.2 Trade-by-trade reconciliation
For every closed shadow trade, emit (backtest_predicted_return, shadow_realised_return, divergence_bps). Divergences > 30 bps per trade require root-cause analysis logged in docs/superpowers/drift-reports/YYYY-MM-DD-<ticker>.md.

13.3 Kill-switch
A strategy with drift_ratio < 0.3 over any 20-trade window is auto-paused until the drift is explained in writing.


13A. REPRODUCIBILITY AND RUN IDENTITY
Objective: Ensure every result can be reproduced exactly and audited after the fact.

13A.1 Mandatory run manifest
Every Sunday referee output and every deployment review emits a machine-readable manifest containing:
- run_id
- strategy_version
- git_commit_hash
- config_hash
- data_snapshot_id — a directory or archive reference
- data_file_sha256_manifest — SHA-256 of every input data file used, stored alongside the manifest
- universe_snapshot_id — points at an immutable fno_universe_history.json commit
- cost_model_version
- random_seed
- report_generated_at timestamp

13A.2 Immutable artifact storage
Backtest report, metric tables, event ledger and manifest are stored together under an immutable dated path. Any regeneration requires a new run_id. Data files referenced in the manifest are pinned by SHA-256 and must be retrievable from the archive even after upstream source changes.

13A.3 Reproduction check
At minimum one deployable strategy is re-run from a stored manifest on a clean environment every month, AND all deployable strategies are reproduction-checked at least once per quarter. Metric drift beyond rounding tolerance is a P1 reproducibility failure requiring investigation.

13A.4 Cost-model versioning
The slippage / cost model has its own version number in 13A.1. Any change to the cost model:
- Bumps the cost_model_version
- Triggers re-evaluation of all LIVE strategies under the new model within 30 days
- Invalidates prior backtest classifications until re-run under the new model
Cost-model updates require principal sign-off logged in docs/superpowers/cost-model-changes/YYYY-MM-DD.md.


14. META-REPORTING AND HYPOTHESIS REGISTRY
Objective: Defend against the garden of forking paths.

14.1 Pre-registration
Every strategy pre-registers its hypothesis in docs/superpowers/hypothesis-registry.jsonl BEFORE any backtest is run. The entry includes: hypothesis_id, author, date, claimed edge, exact universe, exact date range, exact statistical test with alpha, declared hypothesis-family scope (see 14.5). Once registered, the hypothesis is timestamped and git-committed.

14.2 Registry entry lifecycle
Terminal states: PASSED (cleared Sections 1-13 and is live), FAILED (explicit fail), ABANDONED (researcher stopped, must state why), REFRAMED (replaced by a new pre-registered hypothesis, with pointer).

14.3 Multiplicity accounting
The Bonferroni / FDR denominator is the total count of hypotheses with matching scope in the registry at the time of test, NOT the count of surviving strategies.

14.4 Public-facing rejection count
The website Track Record page displays: "Strategies tested: N. Strategies deployed: M. Strategies retired: K."

14.5 Hypothesis-family denominator — primary and audit
Every pre-registered hypothesis declares a primary family scope and (optionally) audit scopes. One of the four is primary and binding for Bonferroni / FDR under Section 3.1 and 9B.4:
- global — all hypotheses in the registry, ever
- strategy-class — all hypotheses of the same mechanism class (e.g., "all residual-reversion", "all correlation-break-follow")
- universe-scope — all hypotheses tested on the same universe (e.g., "all F&O 213 fade-edge hypotheses")
- ticker-family — all hypotheses tested on the same ticker

Primary family is normally ticker-family-wide for per-ticker tests and strategy-class-wide for rule-based strategies. The global registry count is reported as an audit sanity check alongside, but does not replace the primary. The primary family chosen at pre-registration cannot be downgraded later to make a result more significant.


15. DEPLOYMENT GATE — END-TO-END WORKFLOW
Objective: Make the sequence of checks mechanical.

15.1 Gate ladder
RESEARCH → PAPER-SHADOW → LIVE-FRAGILE → LIVE-ROBUST

Progression rules:
- RESEARCH → PAPER-SHADOW: pass Sections 1 (S0+S1), 2, 5A, 6, 7, 8, 9, 9A, 9B, 10, 11B. Pre-registered hypothesis required (Section 14).
- PAPER-SHADOW → LIVE-FRAGILE: pass Section 13 drift audit for ≥ 20 consecutive closed shadow trades with drift_ratio ∈ [0.7, 1.3]; pass Section 3 at S1; pass Sections 11, 11A, 11C; minimum paper duration 30 calendar days OR 50 trades (whichever is later).
- LIVE-FRAGILE → LIVE-ROBUST: 90 calendar days of live with realised Sharpe ≥ 0.8 × backtest S1 Sharpe, ≤ 1 CUSUM trigger, ≤ 2 drift alerts, no Section 11C correlation breach.

15.2 Automatic demotion
Section 12 CUSUM triggers or Section 13 drift alerts demote a strategy one level. Section 11C portfolio-correlation breaches can also demote. Two demotions in any 6-month window permanently removes deployable status for the named strategy — researchers may rebuild, but not re-release the old name.

15.3 Sunday referee
pipeline/autoresearch/unified_backtest.py is the Sunday referee. It runs the full checklist on every deployable strategy weekly, posts output to Track Record / Proof tab and Telegram. If any gate fails, the referee is the single authority that demotes — not the researcher, not the principal, not the UI.

15.4 Waiver register
Any deviation from Sections 1-14 is logged in docs/superpowers/waivers/YYYY-MM-DD-<strategy>.md with reason, expiry date and signing principal. Waivers must be filed BEFORE the deviation takes effect; retroactive waivers are void. Waivers on Section 0 principles are never allowed.
