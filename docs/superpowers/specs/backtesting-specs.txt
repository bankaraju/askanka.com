BACKTESTING STANDARDS — ANKA RESEARCH
Revision 2026-04-23. Governing document for every backtest, paper trade and live strategy launch on the Anka platform. Every strategy must pass every section below before it touches real capital. Any deviation requires a written waiver from the principal (Bharat) logged in docs/superpowers/waivers/.

0. RESEARCH INTEGRITY PRINCIPLES
These are the rules we will not break even when they are inconvenient.

0.1 We do not publish a strategy as "deployable" until it has passed every gate in Sections 1-14 of this document. Partial passes are explicitly labelled "Research" and are forbidden from the live signal path.

0.2 We do not retroactively redefine success criteria to make a losing strategy pass. If a strategy fails its pre-registered hypothesis at Section 14, it is retired — we may rebuild under a new hypothesis but not rename the failure.

0.3 We do not adjust p-value thresholds to fit discoveries. Bonferroni and FDR corrections are applied to the full hypothesis family as scoped at pre-registration, not to the surviving subset. A strategy that stops being significant after multiplicity correction is not significant.

0.4 We do not trust a single-point backtest. Every deployable claim is tested at every slippage level (S0-S2 minimum), across every regime (3 of 4 minimum), on a held-out out-of-sample segment, and validated forward for a specified minimum in shadow before live.

0.5 We do not hide rejected hypotheses. Section 14's registry tracks every strategy ever tested, deployed or killed, with reason. This is the only defence against the garden of forking paths.

0.6 We do not deploy faster than the data can justify. A strategy that cleanly passes Sections 1-13 but fails Section 10's shadow-period requirement waits. "Market-open urgency" is not a reason to skip forward validation.

0.7 We document every decision. Every deployment, waiver, parameter change and post-mortem lands in git with a dated commit message. The principal's ego and the researcher's enthusiasm are not valid auditors.

THE ABOVE APPLIES RECURSIVELY. A tool that evaluates strategies (e.g., the unified backtest itself) must also pass these principles — we backtest the backtester.

1. Slippage stress grid specification
Objective: Measure how robust each strategy and each Phase C class (especially OPPORTUNITY) is under increasingly pessimistic execution costs.

1.1 Slippage levels (bps, one‑way)
Define a grid of additional slippage per side (bps = basis points of traded notional):

Level S0 (Base):

Exchange + broker + taxes as currently modeled

5 bps slippage per side (10 bps round‑trip).

Level S1 (Moderate stress):

Base costs as above

10 bps extra slippage per side (total ~30 bps round‑trip including base).

Level S2 (High stress):

Base costs

20 bps extra slippage per side (total ~50 bps round‑trip including base).

Level S3 (Extreme stress – optional):

Base costs

30 bps extra slippage per side (total ~70 bps round‑trip).

Implementation hint:

In your fill simulator, inject an extra price haircut/markup on each trade:

For buys: effective price = mid/last + slippage_bps × price.

For sells: effective price = mid/last − slippage_bps × price.

Make slippage_bps a parameter; run all tests over [0.0005, 0.0010, 0.0020, 0.0030].

2. Metrics to compute at each slippage level
For each slippage level S0–S3, compute metrics:

2.1 Per‑strategy and portfolio metrics
For each strategy and for the combined portfolio:

Annualized Sharpe (net of all costs).

Annualized volatility.

Max drawdown (%).

Calmar (CAGR / max DD).

Hit rate (winning trades / total trades).

Average R multiple (avg win, avg loss, win/loss ratio).

Trade count and turnover (notional per day).

2.2 Per Phase C class (especially OPPORTUNITY)
For each Phase C label: OPPORTUNITY, POSSIBLE_OPPORTUNITY, WARNING, CONFIRMED_WARNING, UNCERTAIN:

Mean next‑bar (and next‑N‑bars) return.

Hit rate for direction (where applicable).

Annualized Sharpe for trades originating from that label.

Contribution to total P&L and drawdown.

At minimum, you want per‑slippage Sharpe and DD for OPPORTUNITY only, but computing for all labels gives you the informational value picture.

3. Pass / fail criteria under stress
Use your Moderate bar as baseline (Sharpe ≥ 1, DD ≤ 20%, hit ≥ 55%, etc.). Then define per‑level conditions:

3.1 At S0 (Base) – current assumption
OPPORTUNITY must satisfy (per your spec):

Sharpe (net) ≥ 1.0.

Hit rate ≥ 55%.

Max DD ≤ 20%.

Positive P&L and Sharpe ≥ 0.5 in at least 3 of 4 regimes.

p‑value ≤ 0.05 (Bonferroni‑adjusted) for its edge.

If it fails here, strategy is not deployable.

3.2 At S1 (Moderate stress: +10 bps per side)
For OPPORTUNITY trades:

Sharpe (net) ≥ 0.8 (you’re allowing some degradation).

Max DD still ≤ 25% (slightly looser than base).

Edge present (Sharpe ≥ 0.5) in at least 2 of 4 regimes.

Still positive cumulative P&L over the full backtest horizon.

If it fails S1, strategy is flagged “fragile to realistic slippage”; you can still research it but not size it aggressively.

3.3 At S2 (High stress: +20 bps per side)
For OPPORTUNITY trades:

Sharpe can drop, but must remain > 0.5.

Max DD should not exceed 30%.

Still positive cumulative P&L overall (no full inversion into a losing strategy).

If it fails S2 (Sharpe < 0.5 or negative P&L), that’s a hard signal that your edge is dependent on tight execution; strategy can be run only in the most liquid contexts and with conservative sizing.

3.4 At S3 (Extreme – optional)
This level is mostly for information:

No strict bar; use it to see how the edge behaves in “really bad day / illiquid conditions.”

Useful to understand tail risk and to communicate resilience to investors.

4. Reporting / dashboard spec
You already have a Track Record / Proof tab. Extend it with a Slippage Resilience section.

4.1 Per‑strategy resilience table
For each strategy:

Columns: S0, S1, S2, (S3).

Rows: Sharpe, Max DD, CAGR, Hit Rate, Net P&L.

Example row for OPPORTUNITY strategy:

Metric	S0	S1	S2	S3
Sharpe	5.0	3.2	1.4	0.6
Max DD	12%	15%	21%	28%
CAGR	30%	23%	12%	5%
(Values illustrative.)

4.2 Per‑label informational value
For Phase C labels, a small table or heatmap:

Rows: labels.

Columns: mean next‑bar return, Sharpe, hit rate at S0 and S2.

This lets you show that even when you crank slippage, OPPORTUNITY remains unique, while WARNING / CONFIRMED_WARNING still have predictive informational value (even if not directly tradable).

5. Integration into Sunday Unified Backtest
Implementation checklist:

Parameterize slippage in your Unified Backtest.

For each weekly Sunday run:

Run the full walk‑forward once per slippage level (S0–S2/S3).

Persist metrics per strategy and per Phase C label.

Apply pass/fail logic:

If a strategy fails S0 → mark as “Not Deployable.”

If passes S0 but fails S1 → mark as “Fragile,” enforce low cap / research only.

If passes S1 but fails S2 → mark as “Execution‑Sensitive.”

If passes S2 → mark as “Robust.”

Display current classification and last‑run metrics on the Track Record / Proof tab.

If you wire this in, your Sunday referee will not only ensure no look‑ahead, but also continuously answer the question: “Is this edge still strong after realistic and stressed execution?” – which is exactly what a practitioner‑grade system should do.

If you'd like, next step can be: define the exact labels ("Robust / Execution‑Sensitive / Fragile / Not Deployable") and caps (max capital or leverage) tied to each classification so risk management also becomes rule‑based, not intuitive.

6. SURVIVORSHIP AND UNIVERSE INTEGRITY
Objective: Ensure the stock universe available to the backtest on any historical date matches the universe that was actually tradeable on that date — not today's constituent list projected backward.

6.1 Point-in-time universe construction
Maintain pipeline/data/fno_universe_history.json with monthly F&O-member snapshots from NSE going back at least 5 years. When the backtest evaluates a signal on date T, only tickers that were in the F&O list as of T are eligible. Tickers that joined later are unavailable; tickers that were delisted or kicked out between T and now must still appear in historical results up to their exit date.

6.2 Mandatory disclosures per run
Every backtest report must state:
- n_tickers_current (today's universe)
- n_tickers_ever (total unique tickers appearing 2021-now)
- n_tickers_delisted (no longer in F&O)
- coverage_ratio = n_delisted / n_tickers_ever
If coverage_ratio < 10% of the ever-universe OR if the backtest silently uses today's universe only, the report is tagged "SURVIVORSHIP-UNCORRECTED" and strategies built on it cannot cross Section 15 deployment without a written waiver.

6.3 Data provider
NSE publishes monthly F&O membership changes. Maintain a fetcher that pulls and diff-appends to fno_universe_history.json. Watchdog should alert if the file is >35 days stale.

7. ENTRY / EXIT TIMING DISCIPLINE
Objective: Eliminate every form of look-ahead at the trade boundary. If the backtest models a fill at price P at time t, the strategy must have been able to know-and-act on that fill at time t in real life.

7.1 Intraday vs end-of-day execution paths
Every strategy must declare one of the following execution modes and the backtest fill simulator must mirror it:

MODE A — EOD-close entry, close-to-close hold
- Signal finalised by 15:15 IST using only data available at or before 15:15
- Fill price = 15:20-15:29 VWAP (with ±5 bps variance tolerance) in SSF
- Exit at subsequent close (15:20-15:29 VWAP of exit day) or on intraday stop
- No open-snap. No next-morning-rewrite. The ledger price equals what the exchange printed in the 15:20-29 window.

MODE B — Morning-settled entry, intraday hold
- Signal generated overnight or pre-market
- Fill NOT at 09:15 open. Fill price = 09:45 LTP (30-min post-open settling rule) or later
- Rationale: pre-open auction imbalance can move prices 0.5-2% on noise. The 30-min rule eats the auction-noise into the backtest the same way it eats into live.

MODE C — Intraday adaptive
- Signal generated intraday at time t; fill price = t+5 minute VWAP; minimum 30 minutes after open; no fills after 15:20
- The 30-min floor rule still applies

7.2 Forbidden practices
- snap_entry_to_market_open-style functions that rewrite the ledger after price moves are banned in the live path. Only allowed in a labelled "theoretical-alpha" research report, never in a deployed strategy's P&L.
- Using today's close as the entry price for a signal that fired intraday today (because today's close was not knowable at signal time).
- Using adjusted close (for splits/dividends) at entry AND unadjusted close at exit (or vice-versa).

7.3 Audit hook
Every backtest emits an "entry-timing audit" log: per-trade, the timestamp the signal was decidable, the timestamp of the filled price, and the lag. If lag is negative (fill before signal) OR lag < 0 in MODE B/C, the backtest fails this section.

8. STRATEGY-DIRECTION AUDIT
Objective: Detect and flag the case where the live engine fires a trade in one direction but the backtest validates the opposite direction as the edge. This is the #107-class of bug — silent inversion.

8.1 Direction declaration
Every strategy emits, per signal, the intended direction in machine-readable form: { symbol, timestamp, direction: LONG|SHORT, thesis: "peer-follow" | "mean-reversion" | "trend-continuation" | "news-catalyst" | … }.

8.2 Backtest-vs-engine cross-check
The unified backtest re-runs every live-shipping strategy on historical data AND separately runs the opposite-direction strategy for comparison. Report: for the same event set, what is the Sharpe / PnL of direction=LONG vs direction=SHORT? If opposite-direction Sharpe exceeds the strategy's own Sharpe at S0, the strategy is stamped "DIRECTION-SUSPECT" and must resolve before next release.

8.3 Naming discipline
A strategy named "fade-the-overshoot" that internally follows peer direction is a lie. Strategy names must match their direction. Name mismatches are a P0 bug.

9. MINIMUM SAMPLE SIZE AND STATISTICAL POWER
Objective: Prevent tiny-n strategies from being stamped "significant" by chance.

9.1 Per-strategy minimum trade count
- n ≥ 30 trades per regime for single-ticker strategies
- n ≥ 50 trades total for baskets or rule families
- n ≥ 100 events if the strategy is being backtested alone (no multiplicity correction planned)

9.2 Power analysis
Before pre-registering a hypothesis (Section 14), compute the minimum detectable effect size given available n at alpha=0.05 and power=0.80. If the backtest population is too small to detect the claimed edge at 80% power, the hypothesis is under-powered and labelled exploratory only.

9.3 Confidence intervals, not just point estimates
Every reported edge must include a 95% bootstrap CI (10,000 resamples from the event-level returns). A 1.5% edge with a [-0.5%, 3.5%] CI is not a usable signal regardless of the mean.

10. OUT-OF-SAMPLE DISCIPLINE AND PURGED WALK-FORWARD
Objective: Ensure no researcher ever sees the evaluation segment during strategy development.

10.1 True holdout
A segment of at least 20% of the available history, ending with the most recent 3 months, is designated the pure holdout. The holdout data is not loaded into any notebook, script or report during strategy development. The strategy is scored on the holdout exactly once after all tuning is complete. If it fails, the researcher may rebuild the strategy from scratch but the old holdout is burned and a new one must be carved out from even-more-recent data (which typically requires waiting).

10.2 Walk-forward parameters
- Train window: 3 years (756 trading days) rolling
- Test window: 3 months (63 trading days) rolling
- Step: 1 month (21 trading days)
- Embargo: 5 days on either side of every train/test boundary (no data from within 5 days of the boundary is used for either fit or evaluation)

10.3 Purging for overlapping trades
Any strategy that holds positions for >1 day must purge training data that overlaps with test-period events. Specifically: if a test trade opens on T_test and closes on T_test+5, all training trades whose close is within [T_test - 5, T_test + 5] are dropped from training. López de Prado's combinatorial purged cross-validation is the canonical reference.

10.4 Single-use rule for OOS
The holdout segment is scored exactly once per strategy-version. If you re-run it, you must increment the strategy version (v2, v3) and record that this version's OOS was the second touch. After 3 re-touches, the holdout is considered burned and a new one must be established before further deployment.

11. LIQUIDITY AND CAPACITY
Objective: Ensure edges modelled on paper can be captured at the capital we intend to deploy, without the act of trading eroding the signal.

11.1 Average Daily Value threshold
Each ticker's 60-day average daily value (ADV in ₹ notional) must exceed 10× the intended position size. Positions beyond that are backtested with a linear impact penalty: slippage_bps += 5 × (position_size / ADV).

11.2 Capacity curve
For every deployable strategy, report the capacity curve: Sharpe, DD and net CAGR at position sizes of {₹5 lakh, ₹25 lakh, ₹1 Cr, ₹5 Cr} per trade. The curve must be disclosed in the Track Record / Proof tab so sizing decisions are data-informed.

11.3 Hard notional caps
At deployment, a strategy classified:
- Not Deployable — max ₹0 (paper only)
- Fragile — max ₹5 lakh per trade, ₹25 lakh aggregate exposure
- Execution-Sensitive — max ₹25 lakh per trade, ₹1 Cr aggregate
- Robust — max ₹1 Cr per trade, ₹5 Cr aggregate (re-review at this level)

12. EDGE DECAY AND STATIONARITY
Objective: Detect when an edge that once existed has died.

12.1 Rolling edge plot
Every deployable strategy publishes a rolling 12-month Sharpe / Hit-rate / Edge-% plot. The unified backtest refreshes this weekly.

12.2 Regime-change detection
CUSUM (Cumulative Sum) control chart on the rolling monthly PnL with a threshold of 3σ from the strategy's backtest-period volatility. A CUSUM trigger puts the strategy on "decay-watch" for 30 days. Two triggers in 90 days auto-suspends the strategy pending manual review.

12.3 Recent-years requirement
Backtest edge measured on the most recent 24 months must be ≥50% of the edge measured on the full history. A strategy whose recent edge is <50% of historical is labelled "DECAYING" and cannot be new-deployed without a specific thesis for why the recent period is anomalous (e.g., regime-unusual).

13. PAPER-VS-LIVE DRIFT AUDIT
Objective: Catch implementation bugs by comparing shadow (paper) P&L to what the backtest predicted.

13.1 Weekly drift report
Every Sunday, for each strategy running in shadow or live, compute:
- backtest_expected_pnl_sharpe (as of the last weekly walk-forward)
- shadow_realised_pnl_sharpe (since go-live or strategy start)
- drift_ratio = shadow / backtest_expected
If drift_ratio < 0.5 or > 2.0 over a rolling 60-trade window, alert. Either the implementation is broken or the backtest is sham (both are P0).

13.2 Trade-by-trade reconciliation
For every closed shadow trade, emit (backtest_predicted_return, shadow_realised_return, divergence_bps). Divergences > 30 bps per trade require root-cause analysis logged in docs/superpowers/drift-reports/YYYY-MM-DD-<ticker>.md.

13.3 Kill-switch
A strategy with drift_ratio < 0.3 over any 20-trade window is auto-paused. It cannot resume until the drift is explained in writing.

14. META-REPORTING AND HYPOTHESIS REGISTRY
Objective: Defend against the garden of forking paths — researchers testing many hypotheses and reporting only the survivor.

14.1 Pre-registration
Every strategy must pre-register its hypothesis in docs/superpowers/hypothesis-registry.jsonl BEFORE any backtest is run. The entry includes: hypothesis_id, author, date, claimed edge, exact universe, exact date range, exact statistical test with alpha. Once registered, the hypothesis is timestamped and git-committed.

14.2 Registry entry lifecycle
Every hypothesis in the registry ends with one of four terminal states: PASSED (cleared Sections 1-13 and is live), FAILED (explicit fail), ABANDONED (researcher stopped work before conclusion, must state why), REFRAMED (replaced by a new pre-registered hypothesis, with pointer).

14.3 Multiplicity accounting
The Bonferroni/FDR denominator is the total count of hypotheses with matching scope in the registry, NOT the count of survivors. If 400 per-ticker fade hypotheses were registered, any one of them needs p ≤ 0.00012 at alpha=0.05, even if only 8 are reported in the final table.

14.4 Public-facing rejection count
The website Track Record page must display: "Strategies tested: N. Strategies deployed: M. Strategies retired: K." Transparency about failure rate is institutional armour against selection bias.

15. DEPLOYMENT GATE — END-TO-END WORKFLOW
Objective: Make the sequence of checks mechanical, so nothing gets shipped by accident.

15.1 Gate ladder
A strategy progresses through:
RESEARCH → PAPER-SHADOW → LIVE-FRAGILE → LIVE-ROBUST

Progression rules:
- RESEARCH → PAPER: pass Sections 1 (S0+S1), 2, 6-10. Must have a pre-registered hypothesis.
- PAPER → LIVE-FRAGILE: pass Section 13 drift audit for ≥20 consecutive closed shadow trades with drift_ratio ∈ [0.7, 1.3]. Additionally pass Section 3 S1. Minimum paper duration: 30 calendar days OR 50 trades (whichever is later).
- LIVE-FRAGILE → LIVE-ROBUST: 90 calendar days of live with realised Sharpe ≥ 0.8 of backtest S1 Sharpe, ≤1 CUSUM trigger, ≤2 drift alerts.

15.2 Automatic demotion
Section 12 CUSUM triggers or Section 13 drift alerts demote a strategy one level. Two demotions in 6 months removes deployable status for the named strategy permanently — researchers may rebuild, but not re-release the old name.

15.3 Sunday referee
pipeline/autoresearch/unified_backtest.py is the Sunday referee. It runs the full checklist (Sections 1-14) on every deployable strategy weekly. Its output is posted to the Track Record / Proof tab and to Telegram. If a strategy fails any gate, the referee is the single authority that demotes it — not the researcher, not the principal, not the UI.

15.4 Waiver register
Any deviation from Sections 0-14 is logged in docs/superpowers/waivers/YYYY-MM-DD-<strategy>.md with the reason, the waiver expiry date, and the signing principal. Waivers cannot be retroactive — must be filed before the deviation takes effect. Waivers on Section 0 principles are not allowed.
