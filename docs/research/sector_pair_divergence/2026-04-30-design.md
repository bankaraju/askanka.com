# Sector Pair Divergence-Reversion — research design (2026-04-30)

**Status:** discovery-only. No edge claim. No hypothesis-registry entry yet.
**Author:** Bharat (with Claude assist)
**Reads from:** canonical sector panel (`pipeline/research/sector_panel`).

## Motivation

The 2026-04-30 sector-correlation stability study (`docs/research/sector_correlation/2026-04-30-design.md`) found:

- 20 of 22 F&O sectors form one mega-cluster (85% co-membership over 5y)
- Top stable pairs (5y stability ≥70%, |corr| ≥0.5) include:
  - Banks × NBFC_HFC (+0.825, 100% stable)
  - Capital_Goods × Logistics_Transport (+0.808, 100%)
  - Capital_Goods × NBFC_HFC (+0.800, 100%)
  - NBFC_HFC × Infra_EPC (+0.791, 100%)
  - Logistics_Transport × NBFC_HFC (+0.782, 100%)
  - Capital_Goods × Infra_EPC (+0.785, 100%)
  - Power_Utilities × Oil_Gas (+0.779, 100%)
  - Capital_Goods × Power_Utilities (+0.766, 100%)
  - Power_Utilities × Logistics_Transport (+0.769, 100%)
  - Logistics_Transport × Infra_EPC (+0.763, 100%)

**User hypothesis (2026-04-30):** if a pair is normally locked at +0.78, then a day where they DIVERGE materially is statistically anomalous. The market should pull them back together. That divergence is a tradable setup: short the over-performer, long the under-performer, hold for the reversion.

This study tests whether that pull-back actually shows up in the data, and at what magnitude / horizon it pays.

## What this study does NOT do

- **No live trading rule.** Discovery only.
- **No intraday claim.** The canonical panel is daily close-to-close log returns. Intraday divergence-reversion (open-to-T → T-to-close) is a separate, future study that needs minute-bar sector indices we do not currently have.
- **No promotion to live.** A passing result here would launch a registered single-touch holdout hypothesis under `backtesting-specs.txt §10.4`, not a live shadow.

## Hypothesis under test (daily proxy)

For each of the top-N stable pairs (A, B):

> **H_pair:** When the daily-return spread `s(d) = r_A(d) − r_B(d)` exceeds `k · σ_s` in magnitude on day d, the next-day reversion trade `−sign(s(d)) · (r_A(d+1) − r_B(d+1))` has positive expectation, beating the unconditional next-day reversion by a margin large enough to be statistically distinguishable from chance after pair × threshold multiple-comparison correction.

The trade is **dollar-neutral, beta-neutral within the pair**: same notional long the loser, short the winner, hold one trading day.

## Pre-locked design

| Lock | Value | Reason |
|---|---|---|
| Universe | top 10 stable pairs from 2026-04-30 sector-correlation findings | already audited |
| Data | canonical sector panel | per data-validation policy + 2026-04-30 user directive |
| Window | full panel (2021-04-26 → 2026-04-22, 1235 trading days) | maximum n |
| Pair spread | `s(d) = r_A(d) − r_B(d)` (daily log-return diff) | symmetric, scale-invariant |
| σ_s | full-sample std of `s` per pair | locked at study time, no rolling |
| Divergence thresholds | k ∈ {1.0, 1.5, 2.0, 2.5} | grid pre-locked |
| Reversion horizon | 1 trading day (d+1 close) | matches user "intraday" intent at lowest available frequency |
| Trade definition | `pnl(d) = −sign(s(d)) · (r_A(d+1) − r_B(d+1))` | dollar-neutral pair |
| Costs | 5 bp per leg per turn = 20 bp round-trip | placeholder; final live rule would re-cost against actual SSF basis |
| Bootstrap | 200 random 252-day windows, RNG seed `20260430_pdr` | per-pair stability test |
| Multiple-comparison | Benjamini-Hochberg FDR @ 10% across pairs × thresholds | standard, matches autoresearch v2 |
| Verdict bar | post-cost mean pnl > 0, t-stat > 2, BH-FDR survive, bootstrap stability ≥ 60% | conservative |
| Anti-data-snooping | NO threshold tuning post-look. The 4-value k grid is final. | binding |

## What "pass" looks like in plain English (for the findings doc)

> "When Banks and NBFC diverge by more than 1.5σ on a given day (about 30 events in 5 years), buying the laggard and shorting the leader at close earns +X bp the next day on average, with hit rate Y%, and the result holds in 70%+ of random 1-year sub-windows. After 20bp round-trip costs, the trade earns +Z bp, t-stat W."

vs

> "Banks and NBFC are tightly correlated, but the divergence-reversion trade does NOT pay in this dataset. The next-day spread is essentially random conditional on today's divergence. Pivot to intraday."

The honest answer is one of these two. We do not know which yet.
