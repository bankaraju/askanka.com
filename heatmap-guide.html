What is a Spread Trade?

A spread trade is a market-neutral strategy: you simultaneously buy one basket of stocks (the long leg) and sell another basket (the short leg). Your profit comes from the difference between how the two baskets move, not from the overall market direction. This means you can profit whether the market goes up, down, or sideways — as long as your long leg outperforms your short leg.

For example, the spread "Defence vs IT" means you go long defence stocks (HAL, BEL, BDL) and short IT stocks (TCS, INFY, WIPRO). If a geopolitical escalation causes defence stocks to rally while IT stocks lag, the spread widens in your favor.

The 18 Event Categories

Every event detected by the Anka pipeline is classified into one of 18 categories. These span geopolitical shocks and macro/FX regime shifts — the two forces that drive relative sector performance in Indian equities.

Geopolitical 10 categories
escalation
Military conflict escalation involving India's borders or key trade partners. Triggers defence outperformance, risk-off in rate sensitives.
de_escalation
Tension drawdown or peace talks. Reverses escalation trades — defence names pull back, risk-on sectors recover.
ceasefire
Formal ceasefire or truce agreements. Sharper version of de-escalation — markets re-price the risk premium quickly.
oil_positive
Events that push crude oil prices higher (supply cuts, OPEC decisions, supply disruptions). Benefits upstream producers, hurts oil importers.
oil_negative
Events that push crude lower (demand destruction, supply increases). Benefits India's current account and oil-importing industries.
sanctions
Trade sanctions on countries in India's supply chain (Russia, Iran, China). Disrupts commodity flows, benefits domestic substitution plays.
hormuz
Strait of Hormuz threat or disruption. India imports ~60% of its oil through this chokepoint. Acute impact on energy and defence sectors.
defense_spend
Government defence budget announcements or major procurement orders. Directly benefits HAL, BEL, BDL and the broader defence basket.
trump_threat
US trade threats, tariff announcements, or policy hostility toward India. Hits export-oriented IT and pharma, benefits domestics.
diplomacy
Major diplomatic breakthroughs or bilateral trade agreements. Generally risk-on for Indian equities, benefits FII-heavy sectors.
Macro / FX 8 categories
weak_dollar
DXY declining or broad USD weakness. Supports emerging markets, commodity prices, and INR stability. Broad positive for Indian equities.
strong_dollar
DXY rallying or broad USD strength. Pressures EM currencies, triggers FII outflows, hurts rate-sensitive and import-heavy sectors.
india_stress
India-specific macro stress: INR depreciation, fiscal deficit widening, or domestic policy shock. Benefits exporters (pharma, IT), hurts domestics.
rbi_rate_cut
RBI repo rate cut or dovish policy surprise. Benefits banks, NBFCs, real estate — all rate-sensitive sectors.
rbi_rate_hike
RBI repo rate hike or hawkish stance. Pressures rate-sensitive lending, benefits depositors and defensive names.
us_recession
US recession signals (yield curve inversion, jobs data, PMI contraction). Hits Indian IT services (US revenue exposure), benefits domestic consumption.
fii_selling
Sustained foreign institutional investor outflows from Indian equities. Pressures large-cap FII-heavy names (HDFC, Infosys), benefits DII-backed PSUs.
fii_buying
FII inflows into Indian equities. Lifts large-cap quality names and financials, compresses valuation gaps.

Top 5 Patterns — Deep Dive

These are the five strongest patterns from the 4-year backtest. Each one has a clear economic logic and enough sample size to be statistically meaningful.

1. Private Finance vs PSU Energy
92% n=6
Long
HDFCBANK, ICICIBANK, KOTAKBANK, BAJFINANCE
Short
ONGC, BPCL, IOC, GAIL

Event trigger: weak_dollar

When the dollar weakens, two things happen simultaneously: (1) FII flows return to India, disproportionately buying high-quality private financials, and (2) crude oil prices rise in dollar terms but INR strengthens, compressing the energy margin expansion that PSU refiners had been enjoying. The result is a powerful rotation from PSU energy into private finance.

Why it works

Private banks are the largest FII holding in India — when flows return, they are the first to re-rate. PSU energy names are government-controlled with capped upside and lose their tailwind when crude-INR dynamics shift. This creates a structural divergence that the spread captures cleanly.

Historical performance

Over 6 weak-dollar events since 2022, this spread moved favorably in 5.5 out of 6 (partial credit on one ambiguous case). Average favorable move in the first 5 trading days: +4.1%.

2. Defence vs IT
73% n=5
Long
HAL, BEL, BDL
Short
TCS, INFY, WIPRO

Event trigger: hormuz

Strait of Hormuz threats create a dual effect: defence stocks rally on increased geopolitical risk premium and potential order acceleration, while IT stocks (which are dollar-earning but risk-off sensitive) underperform as global clients pull back on discretionary spending amid uncertainty.

Why it works

Hormuz disruption risk is existential for India's energy security. The government response is always to accelerate defence modernization and self-reliance (Atmanirbhar Bharat). Meanwhile, IT companies with US/European clients face deal delays when geopolitical risk spikes. The spread captures this divergence.

Historical performance

Average 5-day favorable spread move: +3.4%. The pattern has been particularly clean when Hormuz headlines coincide with oil price spikes above $85/bbl.

3. PSU Commodity vs Banks
83% n=6
Long
COALINDIA, NMDC, HINDALCO, VEDL
Short
SBIN, PNB, BANKBARODA, CANBK

Event trigger: weak_dollar

A weakening dollar lifts commodity prices globally — metals, coal, and mining stocks benefit directly from higher realizations. Meanwhile, PSU banks are largely domestic-focused and do not benefit from the commodity reflation. The spread captures this divergence in earnings sensitivity.

Why it works

India's PSU commodity producers (Coal India, NMDC) have revenues directly linked to global commodity benchmarks priced in USD. When the dollar weakens, these benchmarks rise. PSU banks have no such linkage — their earnings are driven by domestic credit growth and NIM, which are unaffected by dollar moves.

Historical performance

5 out of 6 events saw the spread move favorably within 5 trading days. Average move: +3.8%.

4. Upstream vs Rate Sensitives
80% n=10
Long
ONGC, OIL, RELIANCE (upstream)
Short
HDFC, LIC Housing, ICICI Lombard

Event trigger: strong_dollar

A strong dollar typically accompanies higher oil prices (oil is dollar-denominated). Upstream oil producers benefit from higher realizations. Rate-sensitive financials suffer because a strong dollar pressures INR, forcing RBI to tighten, which compresses lending margins and housing demand.

Why it works

This is one of the highest-conviction patterns because the cause-and-effect chain is direct and mechanical: strong dollar leads to INR weakness leads to imported inflation leads to RBI hawkishness leads to rate-sensitive pain. Upstream producers are naturally hedged as their product is priced in USD. With n=10, this is also the most statistically robust pattern in the top 5.

Historical performance

8 out of 10 strong-dollar events saw the spread move favorably. Average 5-day move: +2.9%. Smaller per-event but the high frequency and reliability make it a workhorse trade.

5. Pharma Exporters vs Domestic Consumer
66% n=10
Long
SUNPHARMA, DRREDDY, CIPLA, AUROBINDO
Short
HINDUNILVR, DABUR, MARICO, BRITANNIA

Event trigger: india_stress

When India-specific macro stress hits (INR depreciation, fiscal concerns, domestic slowdown), pharma exporters benefit from currency translation gains (60%+ revenue in USD) while domestic FMCG companies suffer from compressed rural demand and input cost inflation.

Why it works

This is the classic "domestic stress hedge." Pharma exporters are natural beneficiaries of INR weakness — every rupee of depreciation flows directly to their bottom line. Domestic consumer companies face the opposite: their costs rise (imported palm oil, crude-linked packaging) while their customers (rural India) cut spending. The 66% hit rate over 10 events makes this a reliable SIGNAL-tier pattern.

Historical performance

Average 5-day favorable move: +2.2%. The pattern is most pronounced when INR weakens beyond 84/USD and RBI signals limited intervention capacity.

Daily Stop Loss

Set at 50% of the average favorable 1-day move for that spread-event pair. Tight enough to cut losers early, wide enough to avoid noise.

2-Day Running Stop

Set at 2x the daily stop. If the spread has been open for 2+ days and hits this level, the trade is closed. This catches slow bleed-outs.
