Built by a practitioner, not a pitch deck

Anka Research is the work of Bharat Ankaraju — a technologist who spent years building AI systems for enterprise before turning the same discipline toward financial markets. The thesis is simple: the tools that detect patterns in unstructured data, model regime shifts, and score management credibility should work just as well on equities as they do in any other domain.

The Anka engine is not a chatbot wrapper or a sentiment dashboard. It is a multi-layer system that combines forensic financial analysis (5 years of annual reports and earnings calls, scored for promise vs. delivery), global regime modelling (overnight macro signals from 31 cross-asset ETFs, ML-optimised), and stock-specific technical fingerprinting (15 pattern types backtested across 74,000+ historical events). Each layer uses a different AI architecture. Together, they produce a conviction signal that no single approach can match.

The entire pipeline — data collection, indicator computation, pattern detection, scoring, and signal generation — runs autonomously every trading day. No manual intervention. No discretionary overrides. The model trades the numbers it sees, not the narratives humans prefer.
