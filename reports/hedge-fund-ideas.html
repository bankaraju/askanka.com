The Spread Trading Philosophy

"We don't predict the market direction. We bet on the spread between what benefits from war and what suffers from it."

A spread trade is market-neutral by design. If the entire market drops 10%, but energy drops 5% and consumer drops 15%, your long energy / short consumer spread still makes +10%. You profit from the divergence, not the direction. This is how hedge funds extract alpha during dislocation events.

Methodology & Sources

Spread Construction

  • All spreads initiated at pre-war baseline (Feb 27, 2026 close)
  • Spread P&L = Long leg return − Short leg return
  • ETF-based where possible for retail executability
  • Index-level spreads use local-currency returns (no FX conversion needed for relative performance)
  • FX trades use spot rates from Federal Reserve H.10 and ECB reference rates

Data Sources

  • Prices: EODHD API (primary), yfinance (fallback) — daily close
  • ETF data: Yahoo Finance, ETF.com, SPDR, iShares
  • FX rates: Federal Reserve H.10, ECB reference rates, xe.com
  • Index levels: Bloomberg, Reuters, exchange data (LSE, Euronext)
  • Fundamentals: Reuters, CNBC, Financial Times, Bloomberg

Limitations & Caveats

  • Spread returns are theoretical — actual execution may differ due to bid-ask spreads, slippage, and borrow costs for short legs
  • Short selling carries unlimited loss potential and may not be available in all jurisdictions
  • Index-level spreads (FTSE vs CAC) cannot be directly traded without futures or ETF pairs
  • FX basket is a conceptual trade; actual execution requires forex accounts with appropriate margin
  • Post-war recovery trade is conditional and has no current P&L — entry trigger has not yet fired
  • One-month track record is insufficient to validate any strategy
