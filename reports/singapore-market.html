Key Finding: Singapore Outperformed Global Peers

The STI fell only ~2-3% from its all-time high of 5,041 (hit Feb 23), while the S&P 500 dropped -5%, Nikkei -10%, and KOSPI triggered a circuit breaker with a 12.1% single-day plunge. Singapore's status as a safe-haven financial hub, combined with strong banking fundamentals and shipping/defense beneficiaries, cushioned the blow. SGX trading volumes surged — SDAV hit S$2.1B in February, highest since 2020.

Sector Impact Analysis

Defence / Aerospace Strong Winner

ST Engineering hit all-time high of S$11.30 on Mar 13. Record S$33.2B order book. Global defence spending acceleration is a multi-year tailwind. FY25 PATMI grew 21%.

Shipping / Marine Strong Winner

Tanker freight rates ballooned 80%+ in one month. Hormuz closure forces rerouting around Africa. Seatrium net profit doubled in FY25 (S$16.6B order book). Yangzijiang's yards full through 2029.

Energy / Utilities Beneficiary

Sembcorp Industries benefits via cost pass-through gas contracts. Palm oil surged 10% in a single day as crude cleared $110/bbl (energy-agri convergence). Singapore refineries benefit from wider crack spreads.

Banks Mixed / Resilient

Higher-for-longer rates support NIM. Safe-haven capital inflows into Singapore benefit all three banks. DBS wealth management fees +29%. OCBC was best performer YTD (+5.8%). Credit quality strong (NPLs <1%).

REITs Under Pressure

Higher rates hurt valuations. War pushes back rate cut expectations from July to September. Industrial REITs with energy-sector tenants relatively resilient. Office REITs benefit from flight-to-quality in Singapore as regional HQ hub.

Exchange / Financials Beneficiary

SGX benefits from war-driven volatility. February SDAV at S$2.1B (highest since 2020). Retail turnover surged 45% YoY. Small/mid-cap turnover up 135% YoY.
