The India Story: Defence Defies the Rout

The Nifty 50 fell 9.4% in March — India's worst month since the COVID crash. But the Nifty Defence Index rose +6%, creating a 15+ percentage point divergence. The war exposed India's fundamental vulnerability: 85% crude import dependence. Upstream producers (ONGC) surged while downstream OMCs (IOC, BPCL, HPCL) were crushed -23 to -25%. FII selling was relentless. The only pockets of strength: defence, upstream energy, and select pharma.

FII Exodus

Foreign Institutional Investors have been net sellers of Indian equities throughout March 2026, accelerating an outflow trend that began in late 2025. War-driven risk-off sentiment, rupee depreciation (-4.2%), and elevated crude prices (India basket crossed $156 intraday) triggered massive portfolio rebalancing away from energy-importing EMs. Domestic institutional investors (DIIs) and retail SIPs have provided partial support, but insufficient to offset FII selling pressure.

Sector-by-Sector Analysis

Defence Strong Winner (+6%)

Nifty Defence Index rose +6% while Nifty 50 fell -9.4%. Mid-cap defence stocks outperformed massively: MTAR +51%, Data Patterns +39%, Bharat Forge +35%. Large-caps (HAL, BEL) rose modestly (+3-4%) but have massive order backlogs. Defence budget at record INR 7.85 lakh crore for FY27.

Energy — Upstream Strong Winner

ONGC and Oil India surged as Brent hit $112+. Every $1/bbl increase adds INR 6,180 Cr to ONGC earnings. CLSA target for ONGC: INR 415 (57% upside). Upstream producers are the clearest beneficiaries of the oil price surge.

Energy — Downstream Crushed (-23-25%)

IOC, BPCL, HPCL down 23-25% MTD. OMCs cannot pass through full crude cost to consumers (political sensitivity). Margin compression is severe. Government may need to reinstate fuel subsidies. Avoid.

IT / Technology Crushed (-25% YTD)

Nifty IT fell ~25% YTD — worst sector. Dual headwind: AI disruption of billable-hours model + war-driven risk-off selling. TCS market cap fell below INR 10 lakh Cr for first time since Dec 2020. Ambit issued Sell on 9/11 IT stocks. CLSA's contrarian "AI fears overdone" triggered brief 4-6% bounce on Mar 18.

Banking / Financials Mixed

Banks hurt by FII selling and growth concerns. However, asset quality remains strong and NIMs are holding. HDFC Bank and ICICI Bank relatively resilient. PSU banks under more pressure due to government bond exposure. Rate cut expectations pushed back to H2 FY27.

Pharma Defensive

Pharma emerged as a relative safe haven. Domestic demand resilient, US generic pipeline intact. Sun Pharma and Dr Reddy's held up better than broader market. INR weakness actually benefits pharma exporters (USD-denominated revenue).

Auto / Consumer Under Pressure

Higher fuel costs squeeze consumer discretionary spending. Maruti and Tata Motors under pressure. M&M relatively resilient (rural + defence vehicle exposure). Rising input costs compress margins across the sector.

Metals / Mining Mixed

Coal India benefits from energy security narrative. Tata Steel and Hindalco mixed — global demand concerns offset commodity price strength. Aluminium benefits from energy-intensive smelting economics (competitors in energy-importing nations face higher costs).

The Energy Divergence

Upstream vs Downstream: The Biggest Spread in Indian Markets

The spread between upstream producers (ONGC) and downstream marketers (IOC/BPCL/HPCL) widened by 30-35 percentage points in March alone. This is a structural trade — as long as oil stays above $90/bbl and the government holds retail fuel prices, this divergence will persist. CLSA sees ONGC at INR 415 (57% upside); BPCL/HPCL have no visible catalyst for recovery.

INR / FX Impact

INR vs USD

The Indian Rupee depreciated 4.2% against USD in March 2026 — one of the worst performing Asian currencies. India imports 85% of its crude oil; with Brent above $100/bbl, the trade deficit widened dramatically. RBI intervention limited the damage but forex reserves declined.
